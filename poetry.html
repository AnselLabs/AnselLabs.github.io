Sands of Time

Sadness will disappear,
Laughter will be near,
Loneliness will be warm,
Hearts will be one.

Innocent like a dove
Are the rough hewn wings of love.
Withered and decayed
as the once noble fledgling
plummets into a sea of sand
— ever descending as rings of doubt
encompass their being.

I stumble in a daze,
following the paths
that shift and grow.
No end to the paths ...
forever stretching
and clawing at the dunes of time.

Night swallows day and day discovers night.
My bare feet grow bloody and blistered,
ravaged by the wilted thorns and distant wails.
I leave a river of shimmering crimson in my wake ~

A final voice echoes
through the barren valley of woe —

Who are you?
I am love.
But what is love?
The opposite of hate.
And what is hate?
The incomplete knowledge of self.

Donelus the Brave

Donelus sat alone on his seat of stone,
And carved and whittled a fiddle of bone;
For he grew oh so lonely
and desired company if only,
For company was hard to come by.
Walk by! Talk by!
So he sat on his stone and dwelt alone.

He had learned the flute and could pluck a lute,
But his little fiddle was a different beast;
For he plucked it and struck it,
For he huffed and he puffed it.
Slammed it! Fanned it!
Yet it seemed his fiddle was mute.

So he sat once again on that solemn throne of stone,
And he told his fiddle riddles and jokes of his own;
For he laughed at his musings
and the broken fiddle of two strings,
Again, silent was his fiddle of bone.

Laugh, bone! Chortle!
Just a little chuckle will do.
Something, bone! Anything!
Or I'll turn you into bone broth stew.

Donelus tried his best
and cast his failed creation to rest,
But his fiddle of bone shook and whirled and spoke —
‘You’re playing me wrong but I’ll teach you a song,
You blow and pluck my strings all day long;
For a bow’s what you need - a bow of your silken hair,
For a bow to dance on my strings of disrepair.
A Bow! Yes a Bow! — now you know.’

He stared at his little fiddle of bone,
Scared and bewildered by magick unknown;
As he plucked and he prodded at his fickle white hairs,
Completing his musical staff of nightmares.

Now he sits on his stone with a friend of his own,
His finely whittled fiddle of bone;
For he grew oh so lonely and desired company if only,
For company was hard to come by.
Walk by! Talk by!
As he sat on his granite no longer alone.

Mood: Crimson echoes

By the Silence

I am troubled,
Immeasurably,
By the silence.

A soldier bails out,
Leaving his entrails fluttering;
Whipping and whirling,
Billowing.

A crystal cottage
Jutting out from the black mire,
Polluted and pestilent.

There was no record of it
Anywhere
In the ancient signs
And symbols.

The dead man's coat,
Green wool,
Sinking into the obsidian muck.

As the pellucid cot
Cuts and claws
And conceals
What the silence
Fights to explain.
