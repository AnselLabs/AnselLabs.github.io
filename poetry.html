Welcome to my collection of poems — a space where words take shape and moods linger. Here you’ll find stories of shadowed worlds, strange souls, and echoes of imagination. Wander through the rhythms, rhymes, and tales that live only on this page.

The Sands of Time

Sadness will disappear,
Laughter will be near,
Loneliness will be warm,
Hearts will be one.

Innocent like a dove
Are the rough hewn wings of love.
Withered and decayed as the once noble fledgling plummets into a sea
of sand — ever descending as rings of doubt encompass her being.

I stumble in a daze, following the paths that shift and grow.
No end to the paths… forever stretching and clawing at the dunes of
time.

Night swallows day and day discovers night.
My bare feet grow bloody and blistered,
ravaged by the wilted thorns and distant wails.
I leave a river of shimmering crimson in my wake ~

A final voice echoes through the barren valley of woe —
Who are you?
I am love.
But what is love?
The opposite of hate.
And what is hate?
The incomplete knowledge of self.

Donelus the Fool

Donelus sat alone on his seat of stone,
And carved and whittled a fiddle of bone;
For he grew oh so lonely and desired company if only,
For company was hard to come by.
Walk by! Talk by!
So he sat on his stone and dwelt alone.

He had learned the flute and could pluck a lute,
But his little fiddle was a different beast;
For he plucked it and struck it,
For he huffed and he puffed it.
Slammed it! Fanned it!
Yet it seemed his fiddle was mute.

So he sat once again on that solemn throne of stone,
And he told his fiddle riddles and jokes of his own;
For he laughed at his musings and the broken fiddle of two strings,
Again, silent was his fiddle of bone.

Laugh, bone! Chortle!
Just a little chuckle will do.
Something, bone! Anything!
Or I'll turn you into bone broth stew.

Donelus tried his best and cast his failed creation to rest,
But his fiddle of bone shook and whirled and spoke —
‘You’re playing me wrong but I’ll teach you a song,
You blow and pluck my strings all day long;
For a bow’s what you need - a bow of your silken hair,
For a bow to dance on my strings of disrepair.
A Bow! Yes a Bow! — now you know.’

He stared at his little fiddle of bone,
Scared and bewildered by magick unknown;
As he plucked and he prodded at his fickle white hairs,
Completing his musical staff of nightmares.

Now he sits on his stone with a friend of his own,
His finely whittled fiddle of bone;
For he grew oh so lonely and desired company if only,
For company was hard to come by.
Walk by! Talk by!
As he sat on his granite no longer alone.

Mire of Woe

I am troubled,
Immeasurably,
By the silence.

A soldier bails out,
Leaving his entrails fluttering;
Whipping and whirling,
Billowing.

A crystal cottage
Jutting out from the black mire,
Polluted and pestilent.

There was no record of it
Anywhere
In the ancient signs
And symbols.

The dead mans coat,
Green wool,
Sinking into the obsidian muck.

As the pellucid cot
Cuts and claws
And conceals
What the silence
Fights to explain.

Decomposition Hymn

Mr Tenebrous, a stubborn yet erudite man
Tall and thin of alabaster skin
Withered and frail and mutilated
After the silence of a world’s decease
Overlooking the fields, forever desaturated
From his forlorn spires of shattered peace

Seeker of truth
And other darker sincerities
The day, most memorable
He fell horribly ill
His insides beginning to spill
His damned infliction incomprehensible

He turned once more
To the tattered pages
Of that ancient azure tome
The ambient echoing of distant drums
As his wording sank to rhymes and tongues
Oh how he longed for home

Clutching his dagger, curved to a point
Mr Tenebrous screaming, his brothers astral thread disjoint
The venoms trickling, homogenizing with the gore
His brothers beloved entrails embellishing the putrid floor

His brain a necrophage
Feeding on the silence of a world's decease
No more than a decrepit skull in a grave
And he craves
To decompose within the void that silence gave

The Collector

Here lie the toppled cities of old,
Whose ancient spires shadow the buried sandstone roads.
And beneath that sea of dust lie treasures of gold!
Scattered in the sun kissed sands, swimming torpid toads.

In a silent land that nurtures ruin and despair
Lives an ailing frail man
Whose sunbaked skin lay loosely on those thin bones bare,
And whose visage petrifying, dirt-caked and bland.

A crumpled keyhole of flesh for his nose,
And sunken depressions for eyes.
A mouth melded shut from which nothing shows,
And crackled skin of sooty charcoal guise.

He wades through the refuse in his tattered burlap rags
Once every cascading sanguine moon,
And he claws through the dunes and the snake riddled crags,
Humming a parched arid croon.

Until the feeble man with no face
Pulls out a mask from the befouled trove of riches,
And dons his new veil with despondent grace.
Annoyed, his furrowed brow twitches.

For year after year he sifts through those forsaken wastes
In search of a mask that finally fits his twisted barren face.
Every wooden facade devoid of his mantic tastes;
Unable to break the curse centuries ago placed.

So he hobbles back home to his cave amid the dunes,
Wherein thousands of masks from lost times of yore
And other strange oddities, caged animals, and boons
Tower high high above from stone ceiling to floor.

Fish that shed light,
Relics of fright,
Three-headed serpents with necrotic bite,
And the masks! Oh, the masks!
All ornate in design,
Yet cracked, broken, sand blasted,
Forgotten, and mine.
Yes, mine! All mine!
For I’m the collector —
And these are my treasures,
My pleasures,
My finds.

Yet I hunger for more, a cure to this curse.
So I slither once more to those sandy sunken spires,
Lit by a flickering scarlet hue terse.
For it’s different this time, I feel drawn by desires ~
The ever shifting sands pull me to stretches unknown,
Echoing chasms and earthen thrones.

And far far beneath the treasures and towers,
A glowing jade mask atop a cushion of fungi and flowers.
I unfurl my slender brittle fingers
And push aside her lichenous matrimonial veil.
Kiss those lovely lips of viridescent mineral.
Hold her darling face up to mine — empty and visceral.
Locking the mask into place,
Our skin melts into one,
A single irrevocable face,
The curse hath been undone.
