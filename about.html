Hello World

I’m an electrical engineer who has been tinkering with wires, circuits, and computers for as long as I can remember. Somewhere along the way, I fell deep into the world of Linux — hopping between Debian, Arch, and whatever else catches my eye — while building a small homelab for servers, media, and experiments that occasionally even work. I’ve also done freelance consulting and co-authored a research paper where our team worked with potassium ion pumps to accelerate wound cell regeneration.

The Artist Side

But it’s not all wires and code. I’ve been an artist for as long as I can hold a pencil. Drawing, painting, digital sketches, mixed media — it’s always been a constant thread in my life. My sketchbooks matter to me just as much as my toolkits and terminals.

Outside of engineering and art, I tinker with coding projects, dabble in game development, write the occasional poem, and set up new self-hosted services just to see if I can make them run. This site is where all of those things — the technical and the creative — get to live side by side.

Looking Ahead

This isn’t just a portfolio. It’s more of a living lab where I post new projects, artwork, and ideas as they take shape. Some things might be polished, others might be half-broken experiments, but that’s part of the fun.

Thanks for stopping by — and welcome to Ansel.Labs.
