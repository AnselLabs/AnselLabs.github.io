City Council Approves Ambitious Transit Plan; Residents Split Over Funding

City

Major vote paves way for light-rail extension

By Jane R. Matthews

T

The city council on Tuesday approved a multi-billion-dollar plan to extend light rail into three underserved neighborhoods — a move praised by transit advocates and criticized by opponents who say the measure risks raising property taxes. Construction is expected to begin next year, with phased openings slated through 2030.

“This is an investment in our future,” said Councilmember K. Lin in a statement following the 6–3 vote. Supporters argue the expansion will reduce congestion, increase access to jobs, and spur economic development along the corridor.

Opponents, however, voiced concerns at a packed public hearing, warning of higher property assessments and construction disruptions. “We need protections for long-term residents,” said Rosa Jimenez, who led a petition drive opposing the current financing plan.

Business

Local startups draw fresh investment to the tech corridor

By Darrell P. Soto

Venture funding has flowed into the region’s tech corridor this quarter, buoyed by follow-on investments in fintech platforms and green-energy hardware makers. Several venture capital firms announced new funds dedicated to seed-stage ventures in the area.

Analysts point to stronger regional support programs and partnerships with universities that have helped early-stage firms refine prototypes and find early customers. “We’re seeing better deal flow and more scalable product-market fit,” said a partner at GreyBridge Capital.

Culture

Open-air film festival returns with classic cinema under the stars

By A. Nguyen

The beloved open-air film festival returned this weekend, filling the park with blankets, popcorn vendors, and a lineup of restored classics. Crowds gathered from dusk until late to watch a mix of international films, all projected on a large outdoor screen.

"There's something magical about watching a classic on a summer night — it brings the city together."

Organizers said attendance exceeded expectations and they plan to expand programming next year to include live Q&A sessions with filmmakers and themed food stalls surrounding the screenings.

By M. Torres

City schools will pilot rooftop hive programs to teach students about pollinators and sustainable food systems. The pilot includes curriculum modules, beekeeper mentorships, and taste tests of local honey.
